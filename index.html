Disaster Preparation

How to Prepare for Disasters

Preparation is crucial to minimize the impact of natural disasters. Here are some key tips for preparing your family, home, and community:

  • Ensure your emergency kit is stocked with essentials such as food, water, medicines, and first-aid supplies.
  • Identify safe areas in your home, like basements or reinforced rooms, for shelter during earthquakes, floods, or storms.
  • Prepare an emergency contact list for your family and neighbors.
  • Stay informed with early warning systems, and ensure you have access to radio or mobile alerts.

Safety Tips During a Disaster

Stay Safe During Natural Disasters

When disaster strikes, your safety should be the top priority. Follow these safety guidelines to protect yourself and others:

  • In case of an earthquake, drop, cover, and hold on. Stay away from windows and heavy furniture.
  • If you're in an area prone to flooding, move to higher ground and avoid driving or walking through floodwaters.
  • During a fire, exit the building immediately and avoid using elevators. Stay low to the ground to avoid smoke inhalation.
  • In case of severe storms or cyclones, stay indoors and away from windows. Avoid using electrical appliances.

Post-Disaster Recovery

Steps to Recover After a Disaster

Recovering from a disaster takes time and effort. Here's a guide to help you rebuild and recover:

  • Ensure that you and your family members are safe and accounted for. Check on neighbors and the elderly as well.
  • Contact emergency services for assistance, if necessary, and follow evacuation or safety instructions from authorities.
  • Assess the damage to your home, and report any major hazards to local authorities for immediate response.
  • Begin cleanup efforts, but take precautions to avoid injuries from debris, downed power lines, or contaminated water.
  • Access government or NGO relief programs, such as food, medical aid, and financial assistance for affected families.

Emergency Contacts

Important Emergency Numbers in India

Keep these numbers handy in case of any emergency during a natural disaster in India:

  • Police: 100
  • Fire Department: 101
  • Ambulance: 102
  • NDRF (National Disaster Response Force): 011-26708000 / 011-26708001
  • National Control Room (For Disaster Information): 1070
  • Red Cross (India): 1-800-233-1234
  • Helpline for Cyclone/Flood Warnings: 1800-11-4222
  • Disaster Relief and Support: 1911 (NDMA)
